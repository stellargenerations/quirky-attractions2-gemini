Craters of the Moon National Monument & Preserve: A Journey to Earth’s Most Otherworldly Landscape

If you want to feel like you're on another planet, visit Craters of the Moon National Monument & Preserve. It's in Idaho's heart, filled with volcanic wonders. This place is known for its moon-like terrain, interesting history, and space exploration ties. It's a must-see for anyone who loves adventure or is curious about the world.

What Makes Craters of the Moon Famous?

A Lunar Landscape on Earth

Craters of the Moon looks like the moon. NASA trained Apollo astronauts here in the 1960s. The park's lava fields and cones make it feel like science fiction come true.

Geological Marvels

The monument covers over 753,000 acres. It has three huge lava fields from eruptions 15,000 to 2,000 years ago. It's part of Idaho's Great Rift, with the deepest known rift crack on Earth.

Unique Ecosystem

Despite its tough environment, Craters of the Moon is home to many plants and animals. Sagebrush, prickly pear cactus, pikas, bats, and even moose live here. This mix of desolation and life makes the park special.

Legends and Local Lore

Craters of the Moon is not just about rocks and lava. It's full of stories and mysteries.

  • The Legend of the Serpent: Shoshone legend says a huge serpent coiled around a mountain here. It was angry at lightning storms, causing the rocks to melt into lava.

  • A “Cursed” Wasteland: Early settlers thought this place was cursed by fire and brimstone. These stories make it even more fascinating.

Tips for Visiting Craters of the Moon

To make the most of your trip to this quirky destination, consider these practical tips:

  1. Plan Your Visit Seasonally: Spring and fall offer mild weather, making them ideal times to explore. Summers can be scorching due to heat radiating off black lava rock.

  2. Don’t Miss Stargazing: With minimal light pollution, Craters of the Moon is a stargazer’s paradise. Check out special astronomy events hosted by the park.

  3. Explore Safely: Lava rock is sharp and unforgiving—wear sturdy footwear and bring plenty of water.

  4. Get a Cave Permit: If you’re planning to explore lava tubes like Indian Tunnel, stop by the visitor center for a free permit.

Personal Stories from Visitors

Visitors often describe their experiences at Craters of the Moon as unforgettable:

  • One hiker recalled scrambling through Indian Tunnel as “magical,” with sunlight filtering through collapsed sections while water droplets sparkled like diamonds on the walls.
